RC UAV with observation capabilities

This was a smaller scale project which involved 3D-printing a gear system on the rear motor of an RC aircraft. The purpose of having a thrust vectoring system is allowing for much greater maneuverability and eventually the ability to create flight control systems where the plane can stabilize itself with a thrust-vectoring system. It operates by having a servo, whose gear is integrated with the first main gear, is attached to a compound gear which leads into the engine-mounted gear. The rear motor is designed to have a vector angle of around 20 degrees in yaw, which means that from the horizontal axis straight down the aircraft, the motor is designed to move left or right 10 degrees in either direction. This was done by doing a 9:1 gear reduction to reduce 180 degrees down to 20 degrees.

The motor ideally was designed to turn 20 degrees but after testing, it was only rotating 5-10 degrees. This was apparently due to the limits of the ELRS open source radio-control software, which only allows PWM inputs of 1000-2000us when the servo I was using had a range of 500-2500us. I simply changed the gear ratio to be closer to 4:1 because the range was around half of originally designed.

Tools & Skills: 3-D Printing, Onshape, Gear Mechanism
